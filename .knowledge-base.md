### Paper I — The Naive Perspective: Numbers as Symbols and Quantities

- **File:** `papers/01-naive/latex/01-naive.tex`
- **Title:** "The Naive Perspective: Numbers as Symbols and Quantities"
- **Series position:** Paper I of 6+1
- **GrokRxiv ID:** `2026.05.naive-perspective-numbers`

**Key definitions:**
- **Definition 2.1 (Numeral system):** A triple (Σ, L, ⟦·⟧) where Σ is a finite alphabet, L ⊆ Σ* a language of well-formed numerals, and ⟦·⟧ : L → ℕ a denotation function.
- **Definition 2.4 (Numeral equivalence):** w₁ ~ w₂ iff ⟦w₁⟧₁ = ⟦w₂⟧₂ across systems.
- **Definition 2.5 (Counting function):** A bijection c : {1,...,n} → X.
- **Definition 6.1 (Structuralist's question):** "What role does X play in the structure containing it?"

**Principal theorems:**
- **Theorem 5.1 (Symbol/object confusion):** Identifying a number with any one inscription is inconsistent with cross-system equations (by transitivity of identity).
- **Theorem 5.2 (No-canonical-numeral):** For any finite set of adequate numeral systems there is a further adequate one not syntactically equivalent to any. (Proof: construct base-b' system with b' = max|Σᵢ|+1.)
- **Theorem 5.3 (Naive Benacerraf principle):** All adequate numeral systems are interconvertible without arithmetical loss; numbers are shared semantic content.
- **Theorem 5.4 (Inadequacy of naive level):** A theory identifying numbers with inscriptions cannot underwrite cross-system arithmetic.

**Indispensability propositions (§6):**
- Acquisition indispensability (empirical, developmental psychology)
- Application indispensability (counting bridges formal ℕ to empirical world)
- Pedagogical indispensability (formal levels presuppose naive facility)

**Bridge to formal levels:** The structuralist's question first arises naively and is formalized five distinct ways in Papers II–VI.

**Haskell artifact:** `src/01-naive/` — parses Hindu-Arabic, Roman, tally, Babylonian sexagesimal into a single `Peano` normal form.

### Paper II — The Set-Theoretic Perspective: Numbers as von Neumann Ordinals

- **File:** `papers/02-set-theoretic/latex/02-set-theoretic.tex`
- **Title:** "The Set-Theoretic Perspective: Numbers as von Neumann Ordinals"
- **GrokRxiv ID:** `2026.05.set-theoretic-perspective`

**Foundational framework:** ZFC (Extensionality, Pairing, Union, Powerset, Separation, Replacement, Infinity, Foundation, Choice).

**Key definitions:**
- **Definition 3.2 (Successor):** x⁺ := x ∪ {x}
- **Definition 3.3 (Von Neumann naturals):** 0_vN := ∅; (n+1)_vN := (n_vN)⁺; so 58_vN = {0,1,...,57}
- **Definition 4.1 (Zermelo naturals):** 0_Z := ∅; (n+1)_Z := {n_Z}; so 58_Z is 58 nested singletons
- **Definition 6.2 (Junk theorem):** A formula provable in ZFC under encoding E but not invariant under E → E'.

**Principal theorems:**
- **Theorem 2.4 (Foundation ↔ well-foundedness):** Foundation ↔ ∈ is well-founded; no x∈x, no ∞-chains.
- **Theorem 3.4 (Peano axioms for vN):** (ω, 0_vN, (·)⁺) satisfies all five Peano axioms.
- **Proposition 3.2 (∈ = ⊂ on ω):** For m,n ∈ ω: m ∈ n iff m ⊊ n. (Fails for Zermelo.)
- **Proposition 4.2 (Junk theorems):** "1 ∈ 3" is true in vN (3={0,1,2}) but false in Zermelo (3_Z={2_Z}).
- **Theorem 5.5 (Peano for Zermelo):** (ω_Z, 0_Z, x↦{x}) also satisfies Peano axioms.
- **Theorem 5.6 (Peano iso):** Unique bijection Φ: ω → ω_Z preserving 0 and successor; isomorphism of Peano structures but NOT as sets.
- **Theorem 6.3 (No canonical Peano structure):** No ZFC formula uniquely picks a Peano structure without encoding choices.
- **Theorem 7.2 (Recursion on ω):** For any X, a ∈ X, g: X → X, unique f: ω → X with f(0)=a, f(n⁺)=g(f(n)).
- **Theorem 8.2 (Initiality of ω):** (ω, 0_vN, (·)⁺) is initial in the category of pointed dynamical systems.
- **Theorem 8.3 (Uniqueness of Peano):** Any two initial Peano structures are uniquely isomorphic.

**Cumulative hierarchy:** V₀=∅; V_{α+1}=𝒫(Vα); Vλ = ⋃_{β<λ} Vβ. Rank(n_vN)=n=Rank(n_Z).

**What set theory contributes:** existence proof (Axiom of Infinity), working metatheory, decidable arithmetic.
**What set theory misses:** structural identity, encoding-independence, native universal properties, univalence.

### Paper III — The Universal Property Perspective: Numbers as Initial Successor Structures

- **File:** `papers/03-universal-property/latex/03-universal-property.tex`
- **Title:** "The Universal Property Perspective: Numbers as Initial Successor Structures"
- **GrokRxiv ID:** `2026.05.universal-property`

**Custom macros:** `\N, \Z, \Q, \R, \Set, \op, \id, \Hom, \Nat, \zero, \suc, \PtEndo, \iso, \eqv`

**Key definitions:**
- **Definition 2.2 (Pointed dynamical system):** (X, x₀: 1→X, f: X→X) in category 𝒞 with terminal object 1. Category is PtEndo(𝒞).
- **Definition 2.4 (F-algebra):** For endofunctor F: 𝒞→𝒞, a pair (X, α: FX→X).
- **Definition 3.1 (NNO):** Triple (N, 0: 1→N, suc: N→N) initial in PtEndo(𝒞). Equiv: (N,[0,suc]) initial for FX=1+X.

**Principal theorems:**
- **Lemma 2.3 (Rigidity of initial objects):** If I,I' both initial in 𝒞, unique isomorphism I→I'; Aut(I) trivial.
- **Lemma 2.5 (Lambek's theorem):** If (I,ι) is initial F-algebra, ι: FI→I is an isomorphism.
- **Theorem 3.2 (Universal property of NNO):** For any (X,x₀,f), unique h: N→X with h∘0=x₀ and h∘suc=f∘h. This h = rec(x₀,f).
- **Proposition 3.4 (Primitive recursion from iteration):** In cartesian 𝒞 with NNO, primitive recursion g(0)=x₀, g(suc n)=k(n,g(n)) is derivable from iteration. Proof via ℕ×X state space.
- **Theorem 3.6 (Equivalence NNO ↔ Peano):** (N,0,suc) in Set satisfies P1–P5 iff it is an NNO.
- **Theorem 4.1 (Up of ℝ):** ℝ is (up to unique order-preserving iso) the unique Dedekind-complete totally ordered field.
- **Theorem 4.3 (Universal property of exp):** exp: ℝ→ℝ is unique smooth f with f(0)=1, f'=f; e:=exp(1).
- **Theorem 4.4 (Universal cover of S¹):** E(t)=(cos t, sin t) unique continuous group homomorphism (ℝ,+)→(S¹,·) with unit-speed tangent at origin.
- **Definition 4.5 (π):** π is half the period of E; equiv, smallest positive T with sin(π)=0 and sin(x)>0 for x∈(0,π).
- **Theorem 5.2 (Streams as final coalgebra):** A^ℕ is final coalgebra of FX=A×X, with out(s)=(s₀,tail(s)).
- **Theorem 5.3 (Coinduction principle):** In final coalgebra νF, two elements are equal iff bisimilar.
- **Theorem 6.2 (Dependent induction from NNO):** In locally cartesian closed 𝒞 with NNO, dependent induction follows from NNO universal property via total-space construction.
- **Proposition 6.3 (Course-of-values recursion):** Derivable from iteration via history state space N×List(X).
- **Proposition 7.3 (Canonical iso of NNOs):** u = rec(0₂,suc₂): N₁→N₂; inverse rec(0₁,suc₁). Aut(N)={id_N}.
- **Theorem 8.2 (NNO as free PtEndo on ∅):** (ℕ,0,suc) ≅ F(∅) where F⊣U: Set⇌PtEndo. Hom_PtEndo(ℕ,-)≅Δ₁.
- **Corollary 8.3 (Representability):** ℕ represents constant singleton functor on PtEndo.

**Coalgebraic dual (§5):**
- Inductive (Cauchy): ℝ = completion of Cauchy sequences of ℚ (initial-algebra flavour)
- Coinductive (digit streams): ℝ ≅ final coalgebra of D×(-) modulo carry-bisimulation
- These are isomorphic by Theorem 4.1 but differ computationally (Type II Turing computability)

**Open problems flagged (§9.2):**
- Complete final-coalgebra characterisation of π and e that avoids inductive HIIT route
- How does F⊣U interact with Yoneda embedding PtEndo → [PtEndo^op, Set]?

### Paper V — The HoTT Perspective: Numbers as Inductive Types up to Path Equivalence

- **File:** `papers/05-hott/latex/05-hott.tex`
- **Title:** "The HoTT Perspective: Numbers as Inductive Types up to Path Equivalence"
- **GrokRxiv ID:** `2026.05.hott-perspective`

**Custom macros:** `\Type, \U, \N, \R, \Z, \Q, \Id, \refl, \zero, \suc, \transport, \ua, \idtoeqv, \eqv, \fst, \snd, \inl, \inr, \Path, \looppath, \base, \Sone, \isContr, \isProp, \isSet`

**Foundational base:** Martin-Löf MLTT_ω (intensional dependent type theory). Three equality levels: judgmental (≡), propositional/path (=), type equivalence (≃).

**Key definitions:**
- **Definition 3.1 (ℕ as inductive type):** Constructors zero: ℕ; suc: ℕ→ℕ; eliminator ind_ℕ with computation rules ind_ℕ P c₀ c_s zero ≡ c₀ and ind_ℕ P c₀ c_s (suc n) ≡ c_s n (ind_ℕ P c₀ c_s n).
- **Definition 3.2 (Numerals):** 58̄ = suc(suc(...(suc zero)...)) (58 applications).
- **Definition 4.1 (Identity type):** a =_A b, generated by refl_a: a =_A a.
- **Definition 4.6 (Transport):** p: a=b in A, P: A→𝒰 gives transport^P(p): P(a)→P(b).

**Principal theorems:**
- **Proposition 3.3 (NNO universal property, internal):** For any C, c₀: C, f: C→C, unique h: ℕ→C with h(zero)=c₀ and h(suc n)=f(h n). Proof: existence by rec_ℕ (judgmental); uniqueness via ind_ℕ on P(n)=(h n=h' n), using ap_f and function extensionality.
- **Proposition 4.2 (Path induction / J-rule):** For C: ∏_{x,y:A}(x=y)→𝒰 and d: ∏_{x:A}C(x,x,refl_x), J(C,d): ∏_{x,y:A}∏_{p:x=y}C(x,y,p) with J(C,d,x,x,refl_x)≡d(x).
- **Remark 4.3 (J vs K):** J ≠ K-axiom. K forces all types to be sets; incompatible with univalence. HoTT keeps J, rejects K.
- **Theorem 4.5 (Function extensionality from univalence):** f,g: ∏_{x:A}B(x), homotopy h: ∏_{x:A}(f x=g x) implies f=g. (Voevodsky's proof via Σ-type trick.)
- **Theorem (Univalence axiom, Theorem 4.2 in paper, cited as Paper V §4.2 in Synthesis):** idtoeqv: (A=_𝒰 B)→(A≃B) is an equivalence. Inverse: ua: (A≃B)→(A=_𝒰 B).
- **Theorem (Contractibility of NNO, Theorem 4.4 in paper):** The type NNO := Σ_{X:𝒰} Σ_{x₀:X} Σ_{s:X→X} isInitial(X,x₀,s) is contractible. Centre: (ℕ,zero,suc,★).
- **Theorem 5.3 (π₁(S¹)=ℤ):** (base=base) ≃ ℤ as types (before truncation). Proof by encode-decode method (Licata-Shulman): code: S¹→𝒰 with code(base):=ℤ and on loop by ua(succ: ℤ≃ℤ).
- **Theorem 5.4 (Hopf fibration):** h: S³→S² with fibres S¹, π₃(S²)≃ℤ via Hopf invariant.
- **Theorem (Cauchy reals, Theorem 5.7 = Booij/HoTT Book §11.3):** There is a HIIT ℝ_c presenting Cauchy reals: h-set with rat:ℚ→ℝ_c, lim operation, path constructor, truncation. Has universal property of Cauchy completion of ℚ. Archimedean ordered field.
- **Definition 8.1 (π as unique existence):** π = centre of contractible type Σ_{p:ℝ_c}(sin p=0)×(p>0)×(∀x∈(0,p).sin x>0).
- **Definition 8.2 (e as unique existence):** e = exp(1) = centre of Σ_{x:ℝ_c}Σ_{f:ℝ_c→ℝ_c}(f'=f)×(f(0)=1)×(x=f(1)).
- **Theorem 8.3 (Transcendence of π, partial):** The type Σ_{p:Poly(ℚ)}(p≠0)×(p(π)=0) is empty. Classical Lindemann proof ports constructively to ℝ_c; gaps: Hermite-Lindemann auxiliary integral, algebraic-integer estimates. NOT fully formalized as of 2026.
- **Theorem 8.4 (Transcendence of e, partial):** e is transcendental over ℚ (Hermite, ported). Same status as π.
- **Theorem 9.1 (Reals unique in HoTT):** Any two h-sets with universal property of Cauchy (resp. Dedekind) reals are equivalent, hence equal as types by univalence.
- **Theorem (Awodey-Warren):** Identity types of intensional MLTT interpreted in weak factorisation systems; simplicial sets model exhibits path groupoid as Kan complex.
- **Theorem (Shulman, §9.2):** Every (∞,1)-topos 𝓔 admits a model of HoTT with univalence; syntactic category of HoTT is conjectured to be initial (∞,1)-topos.
- **Theorem (Cubical univalence, Theorem 7.2):** Cubical type theory (CCHM) proves ua as a defined term; idtoeqv∘ua reduces by computation, not postulate.

**Directed HoTT (§8.3):** Riehl-Shulman simplicial type theory with directed interval Δ¹; directed univalence is an active research frontier.

**2LTT (§8.1):** Annenkov-Capriotti-Kraus-Sattler introduce strict equality alongside homotopical one; two universes prevent inconsistency.

**Modal HoTT (§8.2):** Schreiber-Shulman cohesive HoTT with cohesion modalities disc⊣Π⊣codisc; Riley's spatial type theory.

### Paper VI — The Categorical/Structural Perspective: Numbers as Invariants

- **File:** `papers/06-categorical-structural/latex/06-categorical-structural.tex`
- **Title:** "The Categorical/Structural Perspective: Numbers as Invariants of Structure-Preserving Morphisms"
- **GrokRxiv ID:** `2026.05.categorical-structural`

**Custom macros:** `\Set, \Grpd, \Cat, \Ring, \Field, \Top, \op, \id, \Hom, \Nat, \NN, \ZZ, \QQ, \RR, \CC, \T, \C, \D, \E, \Mod, \eq, \iso, \defeq, \succop, \zeroop`

**Key definitions:**
- **Definition 3.1 (Algebraic theory, Lawvere):** Small category 𝕋 with finite products and distinguished object X such that every object is a finite power X^n.
- **Definition 3.2 (Model):** Finite-product-preserving functor M: 𝕋→Set (or other 𝔼). Category Mod(𝕋) = FP(𝕋, 𝔼).
- **Definition 4.3 (Definable invariant):** Functor I from underlying groupoid of Mod(𝕋) to category 𝔼.
- **Definition 3.3 (T_succ):** Lawvere theory generated by one object X, constant z: X⁰→X, unary s: X→X, no equations.

**Structuralism taxonomy:**
- Ante rem (Shapiro): abstract structures exist independently; numbers are real abstract positions
- In re (Resnik): patterns abstracted from similar systems
- Modal/eliminative (Hellman): statements as ∀-conditionals over possible ω-systems
- Categorical (Awodey): methodological neutrality; structural properties = isomorphism-invariant

**Principal theorems:**
- **Proposition 3.2 (Structural properties are iso-invariant):** P expressible categorically ⇒ A≅B implies P(A)↔P(B). (By induction on categorical formula complexity.)
- **Theorem 4.1 (Initial successor structure):** (ℕ,0,succ) is initial in Mod(T_succ); any two initials uniquely isomorphic.
- **Theorem 4.2 (Invariance theorem):** For definable invariant I and isomorphic M≅M', I(M)≅I(M') in 𝔼.
- **Theorem (SIP, §10.3 of paper VI):** (X,s) =_{Σ_X F(X)} (X',s') ≃ Σ_{f:X≃X'} f preserves s,s'. (HoTT internal statement.)
- **Theorem (Equivalence-invariance, §10.4 of paper VI):** In HoTT, every property P:𝒮→𝒰 is automatically structure-preserving equivalence invariant.
- **Awodey on univalence:** "The univalence axiom is the principle that makes the structuralist viewpoint formally consistent inside a foundational system."

**Connections to other papers:**
- Paper II: two non-equal but iso models (vN and Zermelo); junk theorems; structural invariants
- Paper VI surveys ETCS (Lawvere 1964), mentions Makkai's FOLDS and IZF as alternatives needing development

## Topic Overviews

### Topic 1: coalgebraic-transcendentals
**Full title:** Final-coalgebra characterisation of π and e (codata, bisimulation, dual to inductive HIITs)

**Background:**
The prior series established two competing presentations of ℝ: inductive (Cauchy sequences modulo equivalence = HIIT in Paper V §5.4) and coinductive (digit streams as final coalgebras for X↦D×X, discussed in Paper III §5.2). These are isomorphic as ordered fields by Theorem 4.1 of Paper III (unique Dedekind-complete ordered field), but differ computationally. Paper III's Remark 5.2 flags the tension: "ℝ as a structural invariant lives above the choice of inductive vs coinductive presentation." The transcendentals π and e are defined as unique-existence centres in the inductive ℝ_c (Paper V Definitions 8.1-8.2, Synthesis §7.1).

**What has not been done:**
A complete final-coalgebra characterisation of π and e that avoids the inductive HIIT route. The desired statement: π is the unique element of the final coalgebra νF (for suitable F encoding real-number digit-stream structure) identified by some bisimulation-closed observable predicate. This would require:

### Topic 2: langlands-analytic-number-theory
**Full title:** Connections to Langlands programme, automorphic forms, Riemann zeta in HoTT

**Background:**
The synthesis paper identifies this as "the principal open problem" (Synthesis §7.3, §8 item 2): "analytic number theory — Riemann zeta zeros, the Langlands programme, automorphic forms — has not been substantially reformulated in HoTT. ... ζ(s)=0 as a HoTT-native statement, with ζ a HoTT-native object, remains unrealised." **State of the art (external to series):**

**Formalization in classical proof assistants (non-HoTT):**

Formalizes in Lean 4 / Mathlib: Riemann zeta function, Dirichlet L-functions, Euler product, analytic continuation (riemannZeta_one_sub relating ζ(s) to ζ(1-s)), functional equation, Basel problem (ζ(2)=π²/6), non-vanishing on ℜ(s)≥1, Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions, formal statement of Riemann Hypothesis (RiemannHypothesis: ∀ s, ζ(s)=0 ∧ ¬(trivial zero s) → ℜ(s)=1/2). **HoTT/categorical approach:**
- "Extending the Zeta Function: A HoTT Framework" (2024, ResearchGate) — preliminary/exploratory; redefines ζ within HoTT using univalence, cubical, and higher categories. Not a complete formalization.

**Geometric Langlands (geometric analogue):**
- Gaitsgory et al. (2024) proved categorical geometric Langlands conjecture in 5 papers (arXiv:2405.03599 and sequels). Establishes equivalence between D-modules on Bun_G and quasi-coherent sheaves on LocSys_{G^∨} for a Riemann surface, over characteristic 0. Uses ∞-categories extensively (Lurie's HTT + HA). 2025 Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics.
- This is geometric (over function fields / curves), not arithmetic (over ℤ or ℚ).

**Condensed mathematics:**
- Clausen-Scholze condensed mathematics (2018–2025) reformulates algebraic geometry and functional analysis using sheaves on profinite sets. Provides a 6-functor formalism on ∞-category of analytic stacks (2024). Potential pathway to analytic number theory via condensed modules over ℤ.
- Connection to HoTT/univalent foundations: not established. This would require interpreting condensed mathematics inside an (∞,1)-topos in a way compatible with HoTT internal language.

**What needs to happen for HoTT-native ζ(s)=0:**

Step 1: Define ζ as a HoTT-native object.
- Need: ℝ_c (done, Paper V §5.4), ℂ_c = ℝ_c × ℝ_c as a field (standard extension), holomorphic functions on ℂ_c (requires derivative theory in ℝ_c — Booij's thesis provides scaffolding but not complex analysis).
- Key gap: A constructive complex analysis library in HoTT/Cubical Agda. Booij (2020) develops real analysis in univalent type theory; complex analysis extension is not done.
- The Dirichlet series Σ_{n=1}^∞ n^{-s} must converge in ℂ_c for ℜ(s)>1; this requires formalizing absolute convergence of complex series.

Step 2: Analytic continuation of ζ to ℂ_c \ {1}.
- In Lean (Loeffler-Stoll): done via contour integrals, Mellin transforms, theta functions (3300 lines).
- In HoTT: no formalization of complex contour integration in ℝ_c.
- Gap: constructive analytic continuation. Classical analytic continuation uses excluded middle (identity theorem: a function zero on a set with accumulation point is identically zero). Constructive version requires careful treatment.

Step 3: Define zeros of ζ as a type.
- Classical: {s: ℂ | ζ(s)=0} as a set.
- HoTT-native: {s: ℂ_c | ζ(s)=_{ℂ_c} 0} as a subtype.

- This type is well-formed once ζ is defined; the Riemann Hypothesis is the claim it is inhabited.

Step 4: Connection to Langlands.

- p-adic numbers ℚ_p: constructable as metric completion of ℚ with p-adic absolute value; Cauchy-complete in ℝ_c analogue. Not formalized in HoTT.

**Precise statements needed:**

2. Def(Holo(U, ℂ_c)): holomorphic functions on open subtype U: ℂ_c. [Requires constructive complex analysis]

### Topic 3: etcs-izf-folds
**Full title:** ETCS, IZF, Makkai's FOLDS as alternative structural foundations

**Background:**
Paper II (§8.3) and Paper VI (§3) survey ETCS as a categorical alternative to ZFC. The synthesis (§8 item 3) flags the relationship to Makkai's FOLDS and IZF as requiring development. Paper II explicitly says "ZFC without Replacement is too weak; ETCS is bi-interpretable with bounded Zermelo plus Replacement (McLarty 2004)."

**Three alternative foundations:**

**ETCS (Elementary Theory of the Category of Sets, Lawvere 1964):**
- Axiomatises the category Set directly (well-pointed topos with NNO and choice) without reference to membership
- Paper VI Definitions 3.1-3.2 develops Lawvere theories within ETCS spirit
- Proof-theoretic: ETCS ≡ bounded Zermelo + Replacement (McLarty 2004), roughly ZFC strength minus proper class comprehension
- Structural: NNO is a primitive, not derived from Infinity axiom
- McLarty 2004 ("Exploring Categorical Structuralism"): ETCS provides encoding-free foundations where Benacerraf-style problems cannot arise
- Key theorem (Paper III Theorem 3.5, citing Lawvere 1964): Set admits NNO; plus choice + well-pointedness gives ETCS

**IZF (Intuitionistic Zermelo-Fraenkel):**
- Formulated on intuitionistic first-order logic
- Extends CZF (Constructive ZF) by allowing unrestricted Separation and Powerset
- Strength: comparable to IZF ≈ Z + "ω exists" in classical strength, but constructive
- Key difference from HoTT: IZF is a material set theory (membership-based); HoTT is structural (type-based)
- Matthews-Rathjen (2024): further study of constructible hierarchy in constructive set theories (SEP Spring 2024 edition updated)
- Connection to HoTT: Realizability models of IZF interpret in ∞-groupoid-valued presheaves; some HoTT models satisfy IZF-like axioms (collection, separation) when suitably transcribed
- IZF validates: full separation, powerset, collection, infinity; rejects: excluded middle, choice
- CZF (Aczel's Constructive ZF): weaker than IZF; validated by Aczel's sets-in-types interpretation in MLTT

**Makkai's FOLDS (First-Order Logic with Dependent Sorts, 1995):**
- Preprint: "First-order logic with dependent sorts, with applications to category theory," McGill 1995
- Purpose: A logic in which category theory (and higher category theory) can be formulated such that it is impossible to violate the equivalence principle (only isomorphism-invariant properties are expressible)
- Structure: Intuitionistic/classical predicate logic with weak equality; sorts are dependent (a sort may depend on terms of other sorts)
- Key property: FOLDS-equivalent structures satisfy exactly the same FOLDS sentences; FOLDS equivalence generalises categorical equivalence
- FOLDS signatures: specify dependent sorts; e.g., for categories: sorts Ob, Mor(A,B), and Eq(f,g) for f,g: A→B
- Comparison to HoTT (from Palmgren 2016 "Categories with families and first-order logic with dependent sorts," arXiv:1605.01586): FOLDS is a "first-order fragment" of dependent type theory; FOLDS equivalence ≈ HoTT path equivalence for 0-types and 1-types
- Connection to HoTT: Makkai's FOLDS equivalence notion is related to the Univalence Principle (Escardó et al. 2019); types in HoTT satisfy FOLDS equivalence automatically by SIP
- Szumiło (2019): FOLDS can be extended to an n-level logic whose syntax generalises FOLDS; related to Reedy fibrant diagrams
- n-Category Café (2026): "The Univalence Principle" — Ahrens-North-Shulman-Tsementzis prove the Univalence Principle for various kinds of structures, subsuming FOLDS equivalence as a special case

**Relationships between the three:**
- ETCS + NNO → models of HoTT (via Shulman's result: every (∞,1)-topos models HoTT)
- IZF → realizability toposes → models of intuitionistic arithmetic → models compatible with constructive HoTT
- FOLDS → internal language of (∞,1)-categories → HoTT via univalence
- All three avoid junk theorems of ZFC by design; they are structurally clean

**What the prior series says vs what's needed:**
- Paper VI develops ETCS-based Lawvere theories thoroughly (§3)
- Paper II mentions ETCS, IZF, FOLDS as alternatives but does not develop them
- What's needed: explicit comparison of strength, model-theoretic translations between (ETCS ↔ IZF ↔ FOLDS ↔ HoTT), and identification of which results of the prior series require which foundation

**Open problems:**
1. Prove in HoTT that the type of ETCS structures is contractible (analog of NNO contractibility for the category of sets)
2. Formalise Makkai's FOLDS n-level logic in Cubical Agda and prove FOLDS-equivalence = HoTT path-equivalence for suitable truncation levels
3. Identify the precise HoTT axioms corresponding to IZF axioms (collection = ?, separation = ?, powerset = ?)

### Topic 4: infinity-nno
**Full title:** Higher-categorical structure of NNO; Lurie's (∞,1)-NNO in ∞-toposes

**Background:**
Paper III establishes the NNO as an initial object in PtEndo(𝒞) for ordinary categories. Paper V establishes contractibility of the NNO type in HoTT (Theorem 4.4). The synthesis (§8 item 4) notes: "The corresponding statement for (∞,1)-NNOs in ∞-toposes (Lurie's (∞,1)-NNO) is more delicate."

**The (∞,1)-NNO theory:**

**Rasekh's theorem (arXiv:1809.01734, published TAC 37(13):2021):**
"Every elementary (∞,1)-topos has a natural number object."
- Key result: Unlike in 1-categorical setting, existence of NNO follows from axioms of elementary (∞,1)-topos (does not need to be added as a separate axiom)
- Proof method: Define loop space of the circle S¹ and show NNO can be constructed from it
- Multiple definitions of NNO agree in elementary (∞,1)-topos: Lawvere-NNO, Freyd-NNO, Peano-NNO are equivalent
- NNO used to define: internal sequential colimits in elementary (∞,1)-topos

**Lurie's treatment (Higher Topos Theory, Chapter 6):**
- (∞,1)-toposes are ∞-categories satisfying Giraud-type axioms (presentable, descent, object classifier)
- The NLab notes: "every elementary higher topos has a NNO" with active discussion (last revised 2024)
- Lurie's SAG (Spectral Algebraic Geometry) uses NNO implicitly; explicit definition of (∞,1)-NNO in HTT appears in the context of ∞-toposes with truncation structures

**Contractibility result (Paper V Theorem 4.4, Synthesis):**
The type NNO := Σ_{X:𝒰} Σ_{x₀:X} Σ_{s:X→X} isInitial(X,x₀,s) is contractible in HoTT. This is the HoTT analogue of: the (∞,1)-groupoid of NNO structures is contractible. In 1-categorical terms: any two NNOs are uniquely isomorphic (Paper III Lemma 2.3). The HoTT version is stronger: they are connected by a unique path (which is the stronger contractibility statement).

**Higher coherences:**
In an (∞,1)-topos, an NNO is an object N equipped with:
- 0: 1 → N (map from terminal)
- s: N → N (successor map)
- Universal property: for any (X, x₀, f), unique morphism h: N → X in the ∞-topos
- Higher coherences: the uniqueness is unique up to homotopy, which is unique up to 2-homotopy, etc.

This is automatic in HoTT because of the contractibility theorem. In ∞-topos theory, it requires checking that the mapping space Map_{PtEndo(𝒞)}(N,(X,x₀,f)) is contractible.

**Connection to Shulman's theorem:**
If HoTT is the internal language of every (∞,1)-topos (Shulman 2019, arXiv:1904.07004), and HoTT proves NNO is contractible, then every (∞,1)-topos has a contractible space of NNO structures. This is the ∞-categorical version.

**Delicate aspects:**
- In Rasekh's approach: the loop space S¹ provides the NNO; but S¹ in an ∞-topos is K(ℤ,1) = Bℤ, the classifying space of ℤ; the NNO then emerges as the universal cover of the loop space. This is an ∞-categorical subtlety with no 1-categorical analogue.
- The relationship between NNO and the object classifier (subobject classifier analogue in ∞-toposes) is more complex than in 1-toposes.
- The axiomatics of elementary (∞,1)-toposes (Rasekh 2018): objects, morphisms, finite limits, power objects, object classifier, NNO — and the independence/derivability of NNO from the other axioms.

**Open problems:**
1. Prove in HoTT (or Cubical Agda) that the type of (∞,1)-NNO structures is contractible using only elementary (∞,1)-topos axioms (not Giraud axioms).
2. Characterise the relationship between Lurie's NNO (in presentable ∞-toposes) and Rasekh's NNO (in elementary (∞,1)-toposes).
3. Develop the theory of (∞,1)-NNO for arithmetic in ∞-toposes beyond simple iteration: primitive recursion, course-of-values, and dependent induction in the ∞-categorical setting.

**Key references:**
- Rasekh (2018/2021): arXiv:1809.01734 and TAC 37(13)
- Lurie (2009): Higher Topos Theory, Chapter 6 (∞-toposes, NLab: natural numbers object)
- Shulman (2019): arXiv:1904.07004 (HoTT models in all ∞-toposes)

### Topic 5: directed-univalence
**Full title:** Riehl-Shulman simplicial type theory, complete directed univalence principle

**Background:**
Riehl-Shulman (2017, Higher Structures 1(1):147-224, arXiv:1705.07442) introduced simplicial type theory (STT) — an extension of HoTT with a directed path type internalizing "hom" rather than "path." Types in STT are not required to be ∞-groupoids but rather arbitrary types from which genuine ∞-categories can be extracted using two definable predicates (Segal condition and Rezk condition). The synthesis (§7.2, §8 item 5) identifies directed univalence as an active research frontier.

**Simplicial type theory (Riehl-Shulman 2017):**
- Primitive: directed interval Δ¹ with endpoints 0,1: Δ¹
- Directed path type: Hom_A(a,b) for a,b: A — internalizes hom-set
- NOT symmetric: Hom_A(a,b) ≠ Hom_A(b,a) in general
- ∞-categories as types satisfying: Segal condition (composition) + Rezk condition (equivalences = identities)
- Synthetic ∞-Yoneda lemma proved in STT
- Applications: functors, natural transformations, adjunctions synthetically

**Directed univalence — the missing piece:**
In ordinary HoTT: univalence says Hom_𝒰(A,B) ≃ (A≃B) as sets (equivalences of types = paths in universe).
Directed univalence asks: in STT, Hom_𝒮(A,B) ≃ Fun(A,B) (functions between types = directed hom in universe 𝒮).
This would make the universe of types 𝒮 itself an ∞-category where morphisms are functions.

**Gratzer-Weinberger-Buchholtz (2024, arXiv:2407.09146):**
Title: "Directed univalence in simplicial homotopy type theory"
- Extends STT with modalities and new reasoning principles → triangulated type theory
- Constructs the universe S of discrete types
- MAIN THEOREM: Homomorphisms in S correspond to ordinary functions of types — i.e., S is directed univalent
- This is the FIRST complete example of the directed structure identity principle
- S is "the universe of discrete types" — types whose directed paths are invertible (= ordinary types)
- Proof method: triangulation modality that allows reasoning about the strict undirected structure within the directed framework

**Subsequent developments:**
- Fibrations in Directed Type Theory (arXiv:2604.18668, 2026): extends directed type theory with fibration structure
- Riehl (2025, arXiv:2510.15795): "Synthetic perspectives on spaces and categories" — surveys state of the art
- Cocompleteness in simplicial HoTT (TYPES 2025): defines cocartesian fibrations, closure properties, formalized in rzk proof assistant
- rzk: an experimental proof assistant for simplicial type theory (by Bubenik and others)

**Complete directed univalence principle (open problem):**
The Gratzer-Weinberger-Buchholtz paper proves directed univalence for the discrete universe S. The full principle would extend to:
- All types (including non-discrete types = ∞-categories)
- The universe of ∞-categories itself (where morphisms are functors, not just functions)
This would require: Hom_{𝒮_∞}(A,B) ≃ Fun_{∞}(A,B) for the universe of all types as an ∞-category.

**Significance for the project:**
- Directed univalence is the key missing piece for synthetic representation theory
- If proven: functors F: G→Vect (representations) become morphisms in the universe; sub-representations are iso-monos in directed sense
- Connection to Langlands: automorphic representations are functors from GL(n,𝔸) to ∞-groupoids; directed type theory would give these a synthetic treatment
- Paper V §8.3 already references Riehl-Shulman; Paper IV develops ∞-categorical Yoneda; directed univalence would complete the picture

**Key results available:**
- Riehl-Shulman (2017): STT foundation, synthetic Yoneda, arXiv:1705.07442
- Cisinski-Nguyen (2022): arXiv:2210.08945 (universal coCartesian fibration, related)
- Gratzer-Weinberger-Buchholtz (2024): arXiv:2407.09146 (directed univalence for S)

**Open problems:**
1. Prove directed univalence for all of STT (beyond discrete universe S).
2. Develop directed type theory for ∞-categories of ∞-categories (∞-Cat as a type in some 3-level directed theory).
3. Synthetic ∞-category theory in STT: prove ∞-adjoint functor theorem, ∞-Kan extensions synthetically.
4. Connect directed univalence to Langlands: formulate automorphic representations as directed morphisms.

```
Paper V §5.4 (ℝ_c as HIIT)
    ↓
ℂ_c = ℝ_c × ℝ_c as field  [straightforward]
    ↓
Convergence of complex series in ℂ_c  [Booij + extension]
    ↓
Definition of Dirichlet series Σ n^{-s}  [requires abs. convergence for ℜ(s)>1]
    ↓
Analytic continuation to ℂ_c \ {1}  [MAJOR GAP: needs constructive complex analysis]
    ↓
ζ: ℂ_c \ {1} → ℂ_c  [HoTT-native object]
    ↓
Zeros: {s:ℂ_c | ζ(s) =_{ℂ_c} 0}  [subtype in HoTT]
    ↓
RiemannHypothesis := Π(s:ℂ_c), IsZero(ζ,s) → IsNonTrivial(s) → fst(s) =_{ℝ_c} (1/2)
```

**What's available:**

**Adjacent paths that might work:**
- Condensed mathematics approach: Clausen-Scholze (2018-2025) handles analytic geometry via sheaves on profinite sets; if translated to HoTT internal language via Shulman's theorem, might yield ζ as a condensed-analytic function
- Synthetic analytic geometry: using directed type theory (Gratzer et al. 2024) to give a synthetic definition of complex-analytic functions
