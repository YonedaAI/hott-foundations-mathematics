Imagine a research programme that sets itself one goal — making the Riemann zeta function a rigorous object inside a modern proof assistant based on Homotopy Type Theory — and then maps out exactly which six problems need to be solved first.

That is what this paper does.

Volume I of the Univalent Correspondence series (seven earlier papers) proved something remarkable: if you describe the natural numbers from four different mathematical perspectives — through universal properties, through Yoneda's lemma, through type-theoretic induction, through categorical structuralism — then under a foundational principle called the Univalence Axiom, all four descriptions literally name the SAME object. The natural numbers are unique, not just up to isomorphism, but up to actual mathematical identity.

Volume I ended by asking: can we do the same for the REAL numbers? For the COMPLEX numbers? For the Riemann zeta function?

Volume II takes up that challenge. Six papers, each attacking one piece of the puzzle:

The first paper defines π and e coinductively — as unique digit streams satisfying observable rules — giving a computational foundation alongside the algebraic one.

The second paper builds the real numbers computationally inside Cubical Agda, filling a gap that has been open for years.

The third paper maps the foundational landscape — comparing three alternative foundations of mathematics and locating exactly where the Univalence Axiom makes new theorems possible.

The fourth paper proves that the natural numbers object in high-dimensional mathematical universes is uniquely determined — absorbing an infinite tower of coherence conditions in a single result.

The fifth paper surveys the state of "directed univalence," the generalization needed to handle non-symmetric mathematical structures like the Langlands correspondence.

The sixth paper — and this synthesis — assemble all of the above into a roadmap toward a HoTT-native Riemann hypothesis.

The goal has not yet been reached. But the map is now drawn more clearly than ever before.
