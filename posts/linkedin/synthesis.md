Research programmes gain their power not from individual results but from the connections between them. Volume I of the Univalent Correspondence series established a striking result: four formal descriptions of the natural numbers — through universal properties, Yoneda's lemma, HoTT inductive types, and categorical structuralism — literally name the same object once Voevodsky's univalence axiom is assumed. Volume I concluded by identifying six open problems whose resolution would extend that programme from the natural numbers to the continuous and analytic structures of contemporary mathematics. Volume II takes up all six simultaneously.

"Toward HoTT-Native Analytic Number Theory: A Unified Synthesis of Six Open Problems" is the closing paper of Volume II, drawing together the six component parts into a single research programme. The six parts address: coinductive characterisations of the transcendentals π and e (Part I), a complete cubical Agda formalisation of the Cauchy reals (Part II), a comparative analysis of the foundational systems ETCS, IZF, and FOLDS relative to HoTT (Part III), contractibility of the type of (∞,1)-categorical natural number objects (Part IV), the current state of directed univalence (Part V), and a roadmap for HoTT-native analytic number theory and the Riemann zeta function (Part VI). Each part was designed to address exactly one link in the prerequisite chain that any HoTT-native definition of ζ(s) requires.

The synthesis paper extracts five cross-cutting themes: inductive-coinductive duality, structural versus material foundations and the univalence boundary between them, coherence and contractibility as bridges from 1-categorical to (∞,1)-categorical universality, the symmetric-directed contrast in univalence, and the ζ-prerequisite chain itself. It provides a single mathematical artifact — an (∞,1)-topos with directed-univalent structure presheaves and a Riemann-zeta object as a synthetic distribution — in which all six contributions find their place.

The synthesis underscores that the six parts are not independent papers sharing a theme: removing any one leaves a perceptible gap in the chain toward the stated goal, a HoTT-native proposition ζ(s)=0. That goal remains open. This series advances the research frontier and maps the territory more precisely than has been done before.
